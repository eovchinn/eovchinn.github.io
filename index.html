Katja Ovchinnikova

computer scientist and climate activist

I currently work on AI-driven precision medicine for cancer treatment at the Department for BioMedical Research of the University of Bern. On a volunteer basis, I work on computational analysis of marine science data. I'm also part of Homeward Bound, a leadership program for women in STEMM focusing on climate change. For more details, please check my blog and twitter @Katja_HB4.

Current projects

AI-driven precision medicine

Precision medicine for cancer treatment. I work on applying deep learning and other computer science algorithms to genomic, proteomic and molecular data.

team website
METASPACE

Computational metabolite annotation of imaging mass spectrometry data. I work on applying natural language processing and computer vision algorithms to metabolite data.

project website
Coral health

Statistical analysis of the lesion distribution to describe coral disease and computer vision analysis of the imagery of coral colonies. In collaboration with UH Hilo Hawaii.

coral data
Scallop detection

Automatic detection of scallops in sea bottom imagery. In collaboration with University of St. Andrews.

Past projects

XPERIENCE

Xperience implements, adapts, and extends a complete robot system for automating introspective, predictive, and interactive understanding of actions and dynamic situations. I worked on a dialog system, planning, and domain-specific knowledge extraction from corpora.

project website
Photo ID of manta rays

Development of an algorithm and a desktop application for automatic identification of manta ray images. In collaboration with Hawaii Association for Marine Education & Research.

manta ray data
Photo ID of blue whales

Prototype project on developing of a web service for uploading and annotating photographs of whales, matching new images against the accumulated whale catalogue, acquiring statistics about whale migration paths and population distributions.


Heider-Simmel Interactive Theater

Interpretation and narrativization of simple two-dimensional videos in terms of goals, plans, beliefs, and emotions. I worked on application of probabilistic abduction to scene recognition.

project website
Metaphor Interpretation for Cultural Schemas

Detection of linguistic metaphors in English, Spanish, Russian, and Farsi texts, and mapping of them to conceptual metaphors. I supervised a group responsible for development of an abduction-based metaphor interpretation pipeline and corpus-based knowledge extraction.

github repo
Grammar Formalisms and Parsing

Online course for teaching grammar formalisms and parsing. I developed the MoMo graphical tool for visualizing formal grammars.

course materials

Adaptive Ontologies on extreme Markup Structures

Automatic extraction of ontological knowledge from text and updating existing knowledge bases. I developed algorithms for non-monotonic ontology updates including automatic resolution of ontological inconsistencies.

project website
Distributional Idiosyncrasies

Study of how syntactic, semantic, and selectional properties constraint lexical elements in their contexts of use. I worked on developing a similarity measure for comparing grammatical structures and on implementing a web service for psycholinguistic experiments.

project website
Machine Reading

Acquisition of knowledge by machine reading. I worked on turning lexical-semantic databases like WordNet and FrameNet into machine-readable FOL axioms applicable for reasoning.
